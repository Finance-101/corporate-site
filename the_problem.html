Addressing the Crucial Issue of Financial Literacy

As we navigate the complex landscape of the modern world, the imperative to address financial literacy has emerged as a pressing national necessity, and honestly also globally. It is particularly poignant when focusing on the younger generations, who, despite their expressed interest in financial matters, may find themselves lacking the essential and desired knowledge in the intricate realm of finance.

The rapidly evolving financial landscape demands a new level of understanding, and it is our unwavering commitment at Finance 101 to bridge the gap between curiosity and comprehension. We recognize that empowering individuals with the right financial knowledge is key to fostering a financially literate society, paving the way for informed decisions and sustainable economic well-being.

Our initiative is not just about imparting theoretical knowledge; it's about equipping individuals with practical insights that empower them to make sound financial choices. Whether it's understanding the basics of budgeting, navigating the complexities of investments, or preparing for financial milestones, Finance 101 is dedicated to providing comprehensive education that is both accessible and impactful.

Join us on this transformative journey as we strive to create a future where financial literacy is not just a skill but a fundamental aspect of personal and societal success. Together, we can empower individuals to navigate the intricacies of the financial world, ensuring a brighter and more secure future for generations to come.

Financial Literacy Statistics

The 2018 PISA study revealed a concerning statistic: only 29% of 15-year-old Portuguese students reached or surpassed level 4 (the highest) of financial proficiency. This places Portugal below the OECD average of 32%.

In the S&P Global FinLit survey, it was also found that only 26% of the adult population in Portugal between the ages of 15 and 34 is financially literate.

Expanding the perspective to Europe, the survey highlighted that only 18% of European citizens possess a high level of financial literacy, while 64% have an intermediate level, and 18% have a reduced level.
